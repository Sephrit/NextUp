# Rating model

Next Up uses a layered rating instead of asking one number to do everything:

1. **Half-star score (0.5–5):** quick comparison and ranking.
2. **Would rewatch:** separates “good once” from long-term favorites.
3. **Favorite:** a deliberately rare personal shelf signal.
4. **Short review:** preserves why the person felt that way at that moment.
5. **Per-watch entry:** a rewatch can receive a new verdict without erasing the old one.

For groups, each person submits only their own rating. The recommended reveal
mode hides scores, review text, and derived averages until every current profile
has submitted for that watch. An immediate mode is available for solo viewing
or groups that do not want the game-like reveal.

Future comparisons should show median as well as mean for larger groups, plus
rating spread. Median resists one extreme vote; spread is useful because a
divisive two-star/five-star movie is different from a unanimous 3.5.
